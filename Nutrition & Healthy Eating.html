Section 3

Principles of Healthy Eating

  • Variety: Eat diverse foods from all food groups daily.
  • Adequacy: Provide sufficient nutrients and energy for body needs.
  • Balance: Proportionate intake of macronutrients and micronutrients.
  • Moderation: Limit added sugars, salt, and saturated/trans fats.
  • Nutrient Density: Prefer foods high in nutrients but lower in calories.

Section 4

Food Guides

Use models such as the Food Pyramid or Healthy Eating Plate to plan meals. Emphasize:

  • Whole grains and fibre‚Äërich foods
  • Fruits and vegetables (rich in vitamins, minerals, antioxidants)
  • Lean proteins like fish, poultry, legumes
  • Healthy fats from vegetable oils, nuts, seeds
  • Dairy or calcium‚Äërich alternatives
Limit red meat, processed foods, added sugars, and excess salt to reduce risk of obesity, cardiovascular diseases, and diabetes.

Section 6

Nutrition & Disease Prevention

  • Obesity: Energy balance; portion control; high fibre; limit ultra‚Äëprocessed foods.
  • Type 2 Diabetes: Choose low‚ÄëGI carbs; include legumes; consistent meal timing; active lifestyle.
  • Hypertension: Reduce sodium; prefer fresh foods; increase potassium (fruits/vegetables).
  • Cardiovascular disease: Emphasize unsaturated fats (nuts, seeds, fish); whole grains; soluble fibre.
  • Certain cancers: More colourful vegetables & fruits; maintain healthy weight; avoid charred/processed meats.
Physical activity complements nutrition‚Äîcombine aerobic + strength activities for best outcomes.

Food Safety

  • Personal hygiene: wash hands; clean utensils & surfaces.
  • Separate raw and cooked foods to avoid cross‚Äëcontamination.
  • Cook foods thoroughly; reheat properly.
  • Refrigerate perishable foods promptly; follow first‚Äëin, first‚Äëout.
  • Use safe water & raw materials; avoid adulterated/contaminated foods.
