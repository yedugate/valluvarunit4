Overview

Managing blood pressure through diet helps reduce the risk of hypertension and cardiovascular disease. A heart‚Äëhealthy eating pattern emphasises nutrient‚Äërich, minimally processed foods to support vascular health and regulate blood pressure.

Combine dietary changes with regular physical activity, weight management, sleep hygiene, and reduced alcohol to maximise BP improvement.
Section 2

Key Nutritional Principles for Blood Pressure Control

  • Increase potassium, magnesium, calcium: Support vasodilation and sodium balance.
  • Limit sodium (salt): Excess sodium raises BP via fluid retention.
  • Emphasise dietary fibre: Particularly soluble fibre to support lipid and endothelial health.
  • Choose healthy fats: Prioritise MUFA/PUFA to improve endothelial function and inflammation.
  • Antioxidants & polyphenols: From plants, tea, cocoa to enhance nitric oxide and reduce oxidative stress.
