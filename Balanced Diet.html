Key Features

  • Correct proportions (general guidance): Carbohydrates 50‚Äì60% of total calories; Proteins 10‚Äì15%; Fats 20‚Äì30%.

Section 3

Components of a Balanced Diet

  • Carbohydrates: Energy source. Foods: grains, rice, bread, pasta, fruits, vegetables.
  • Proteins: Growth, repair, enzymes/hormones. Foods: pulses, legumes, meat, eggs, dairy, tofu, nuts.
  • Fats (mostly unsaturated): Energy, cell function, absorption of fat‚Äësoluble vitamins. Foods: vegetable oils, nuts, fatty fish, seeds.
  • Vitamins: Regulation of metabolic processes. Foods: fruits, vegetables, dairy, meat, eggs.
  • Minerals: Structure & regulation (e.g., calcium, iron, iodine). Foods: leafy greens, dairy, nuts, seafood.
  • Dietary fibre: Gut health & digestion. Foods: whole grains, pulses, fruits, vegetables.
  • Water: Hydration and transport for all body processes.
Combine plant proteins (e.g., cereals + pulses) across the day to ensure a complete amino acid profile in vegetarian diets.

Section 6

Steps for Planning a Balanced Diet

  1. Assess needs: age, sex, physical activity, health status.
  2. Select foods: include all groups in the right amounts.
  3. Control portions: avoid overeating or excessive restriction.
  4. Include variety: rotate foods within groups for full nutrient spectrum.
  5. Limit discretionary foods: reduce high sugar/salt/unhealthy fats and alcohol.

Section 8

Importance of a Balanced Diet

  • Supports growth, immunity, cognitive and motor performance (vital for students).
  • Reduces risk of malnutrition and chronic diseases: obesity, diabetes, heart disease, anaemia.
  • Promotes healthy weight and overall well‚Äëbeing.
Section 9

Exam Notes: Key Points to Remember

  • Definition and principles of a balanced diet.
  • Recall proportions and roles of all nutrient groups.
  • Explain functions and major sources for each nutrient.
  • Recognize pitfalls: excess/deficit of nutrients and health effects.
  • Recall portion sizes, examples for each food group, and sample menus.
  • Apply knowledge to different ages, activity levels, and special conditions.
