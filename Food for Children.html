Importance of Nutrition in Childhood

Childhood is a critical period of rapid growth and development requiring adequate nutrients for physical growth, brain development, immunity, and well‚Äëbeing.

  • Balanced nutrition supports cognitive function, memory, and learning ability in school‚Äëaged children.
  • Poor nutrition can impair growth and immunity and increase the risk of chronic diseases later in life.
Encourage regular meals and healthy snacks; include breakfast to support concentration and steady energy at school.

Principles of a Healthy Diet for Children

  • Include foods from all 5 groups: fruits; vegetables; grains (prefer whole grains); protein foods; dairy or alternatives.
  • Emphasise nutrient‚Äëdense foods low in added sugars, saturated fats, and salt.
  • Encourage variety and ‚Äúeat the rainbow‚Äù with colourful fruits and vegetables.
  • Limit processed foods, sugary snacks, and sweetened beverages.
  • Promote regular meal patterns including breakfast to support cognition.
