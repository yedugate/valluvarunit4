Section 1

Definition

Dietary fiber refers to indigestible carbohydrates from plant foods that pass through the gastrointestinal tract largely intact. It is commonly categorised into soluble and insoluble fiber, each with distinct physiological effects.

Increase fiber gradually and drink adequate fluids to minimise GI discomfort while the gut adapts.

Section 6

Teaching Points

  • Differentiate soluble vs insoluble fiber and their functions.
  • Encourage whole, minimally processed plant foods daily.
  • Practical boosts: swap to whole grains; add a legume serving; include fruit/veg at every meal.
  • Recognise symptoms of inadequate fiber (constipation, irregularity) and the need for fluids.
