Section 1

Overview

A diabetic diet aims to control blood glucose by eating healthy meals at regular times and prioritising nutrient‚Äëdense foods that support glucose control, healthy weight, and cardiovascular risk reduction.

Regular physical activity (aerobic + resistance) improves insulin sensitivity and complements dietary management.
Section 2

Key Principles

  • Balance carbohydrate intake: Prefer complex, low‚ÄëGI carbohydrates to blunt glucose spikes.
  • Regular meal timing: Helps optimise insulin action and blood glucose control.
  • Increase fibre: Soluble and insoluble fibre slow absorption and support heart health.
  • Choose healthy fats: Emphasise MUFA/PUFA from nuts, seeds, fish, and plant oils.
  • Limit added sugars & refined grains: Reduce rapid post‚Äëmeal rises.
  • Moderate protein: Lean sources support satiety and muscle; adjust if kidney disease.
  • Hydrate with water: Avoid sugar‚Äësweetened beverages.

Section 4

Foods to Limit or Avoid

  • Sugary drinks and fruit juices
  • Processed snacks and sweets
  • Refined carbohydrates (white bread, white rice)
  • Saturated and trans fats (fried foods, fatty cuts of meat, hydrogenated oils)
  • Excess salt (manage blood pressure)

Section 8

Practical Tips for Teaching

  • Encourage meal planning with portion control (use the Diabetes Plate visual).
  • Teach carbohydrate counting and glycaemic index/load.
  • Discuss weight management and the role of physical activity.
  • Use culturally relevant foods and cooking methods.
  • Address label reading: total carbs, fibre, added sugars, sodium, fats.
Section 9

Exam Focus Points
